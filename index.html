02

Approach

How I think in the theatre — and beyond it.

Clinical philosophy

Clarity before incision.

Every case begins with a disciplined read of the signal: anatomy, pain pattern, imaging, and the patient’s own thresholds for risk. Minimally invasive and endoscopic technique is a means — not a slogan — chosen when it protects tissue, recovery, and long-term motion without compromising safety.

Pain & recovery

Surgery is one chapter of care.

Interventional spine thinking and structured pain therapy shape how I plan pre- and post-operatively. The goal is durable function: less pain, clearer decisions, and a path back to ordinary life that patients can understand and trust.

Clinical innovation

Digital tools that earn their place in care.

Augmented reality, AI-assisted documentation, and GDPR-aware workflow design only matter if they reduce friction for surgeons and protect patients. My MHBA thesis on AR in healthcare and day-to-day work with clinical AI keep that standard practical: better signal, less noise, no theatre of tech for its own sake.

“Technical discipline matters most when paired with resilience, clarity, and compassion.”

— shaped by clinical work in Germany and humanitarian service with Médecins Sans Frontières
